🐄 The Bioscience Problem

Optimizing marketing ages and feed efficiency for native Kedah-Kelantan (KK) cattle is vital for Malaysian livestock sustainability. Using a realistic sigmoidal growth model, we track the average live weight of a KK bull over its first 30 months.

By applying the first derivative $f'(x)$, we can pinpoint the exact moment of the maximum growth rate (the inflection point), showing exactly when the animal is putting on meat fastest.

📐 Mathematical Model

Growth Function $f(x)$ (Logistic Model):
$$f(x) = \frac{300}{1 + 9 \cdot e^{-0.18x}}$$
$f(x)$ = Weight in kg, $x$ = Age in months
First Derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Gain):
$$f'(x) = \frac{486 \cdot e^{-0.18x}}{(1 + 9 \cdot e^{-0.18x})^2}$$
$f'(x)$ = Growth rate in kg/month
Data Context & Web References: Model constants are adapted to match mature weights and growth curves typical of Malaysian KK cattle benchmarks published in:
  • Malaysian Journal of Animal Science (MJAS)
  • MARDI Research Journal (Cattle Breeding Systems)
